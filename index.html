02 — Profile

I didn't plan to be an operator.
I just couldn't leave broken things alone.

My family moved to San Francisco from China when I was one. I grew up watching my parents navigate a country whose systems — healthcare, banking, bureaucracy — weren't designed for them. I became the translator. The fixer. The person who read the fine print.

That instinct followed me to UC San Diego, where I was the first in my family to go to college. At Rady, I kept gravitating to the same problems: the spreadsheet no one maintained, the process no one documented, the gap between how things were supposed to work and how they actually did.

At Freewire Technologies and Marlo.Today, that instinct became the work: audit what is scattered, make the operating picture visible, and build a process the team can keep using.

I'm most useful when a team is moving fast enough to feel the chaos but hasn't yet built the infrastructure to survive it.
